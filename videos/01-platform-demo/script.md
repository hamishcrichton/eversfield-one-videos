# Eversfield One — Platform Demo Video Script

**Video Title:** Eversfield One: Fire Door Compliance, Simplified  
**Duration:** ~6:30  
**Audience:** Prospective clients (building managers, housing associations, fire safety officers)  
**Tone:** Knowledgeable, conversational, British English  
**Voice:** AI-generated British male (ElevenLabs)

**Visual:** Character standing in front of a modern residential building, clipboard in hand, looking concerned. Camera slowly pulls back.

**Narration:**

**Visual:** Screen recording of the Fire Doors Dashboard. Slow scroll through KPI cards, then the analytics section with Inspection Trends, Common Failures, and Scheme Breakdown charts.

## Section 4: Starting an Inspection (1:30–2:00)

**Visual:** Screen recording of the inspection wizard. Show clicking "New Inspection" card, then Step 1 (Select Door) — searching for a door, selecting it. Then Step 2 (Setup) — filling in date, type, inspector name.

**Narration:**

> Starting an inspection takes seconds. Select your door — you can search by reference, location, or scheme. Choose your inspection type: Routine, Post-Remediation, or Installation. Set the date, and you're straight into the checklist.
>
> If there's a previous survey on record, you'll see the last inspector's notes and recommendation right here — so nothing gets missed between visits.

**[Duration: ~25 seconds, ~55 words]**

## Section 5: The Checklist (2:00–2:30)

**Visual:** Screen recording of Step 3 (Checklist). Show scrolling through check items organised by category, tapping Pass/Fail on items, capturing a photo, adding a measurement.

**Narration:**

> The checklist is tailored to each door type. Items are grouped by category, and for each one you simply mark Pass, Fail, or Not Applicable.
>
> Need to capture evidence? Tap the camera icon and the photo is attached to that specific check item. Some items require measurements — the app validates them in real time so nothing gets submitted incomplete.

**[Duration: ~25 seconds, ~55 words]**

## Section 6: Completing the Inspection (2:30–3:00)

**Visual:** Screen recording of Step 4 (Summary). Show the results breakdown — Pass/Fail/N/A counts, overall result badge. Then the Submit button. Then briefly show the offline sync indicator.

**Narration:**

> When you're done, the summary shows your results at a glance: how many items passed, how many failed, and the overall outcome.
>
> Hit submit and it's recorded instantly. And here's the key: this entire workflow works offline. No signal on site? No problem. The inspection queues locally and syncs automatically when you're back online.

**[Duration: ~25 seconds, ~55 words]**

## Production Notes

### Total Word Count: ~625 words
At a comfortable narration pace (~100 words/minute), this runs approximately 6:15 — within our 5-7 minute target.

### Screen Recording Timing
Each screen recording should be captured with ~30% extra footage to allow trimming in post. Smooth, deliberate mouse movements. Pause 1-2 seconds on key UI elements.

### Character Overlay Placement
- **Sections 1-2, 9, 13:** Character full-frame or large
- **Sections 3-8, 10-12:** Character as picture-in-picture, lower-right, ~25% frame width
- **Section 12:** No PiP — voice only over rapid screen cuts

### Music
Low ambient/corporate track throughout. Fade in during Section 1, slight lift during Section 9 transition, subtle energy increase for Section 12 montage, warm resolve for Section 13.
